# MOWC Player Guide

MOWC is an offline-first companion for *Monster of the Week*, a tabletop RPG where you and your friends play as hunters investigating supernatural mysteries.

## Getting Started

### Register and Log In

Visit the app and create an account. You'll need an email and password. Keep your credentials secure—you're the only one who can access your account.

### Join a Campaign

Once logged in, you'll see a "My Campaigns" screen. Your Keeper (the game master) will provide an invitation code. Click "Join Campaign," enter the code, and you're in.

Alternatively, if you already have a character in a campaign, you can navigate directly to the campaign from the "My Campaigns" list.

## Building Your Character

When you join a campaign, create your hunter by clicking "New Character":

1. **Choose your playbook.** Your Keeper has loaded content packs into the campaign. Select the playbook that fits your character concept (e.g., The Hardened, The Crooked, The Mundane).

2. **Pick your ratings line.** Your playbook offers 2-3 options (e.g., Charm/Cool/Sharp/Tough/Weird). Choose the one that matches your hunter's strengths.

3. **Select your looks.** A few options for appearance; pick the one that feels right.

4. **Choose your moves.** You'll get one move and a list of others to pick from. Moves are the special things your hunter can do in a crisis.

5. **Select gear.** Your playbook gives you a list of starting gear options. Pick items that fit your character.

6. **Name and review.** Give your hunter a name, read through your character sheet, and save.

Once saved, your character appears in your campaign's character list. You can come back anytime to edit it.

## Your Character Sheet

Your character sheet is your home base. It shows:

- **Ratings**: Your five core stats (Charm, Cool, Sharp, Tough, Weird). These range from -1 to +3 and determine how well you do things.
- **Luck track**: A row of boxes. When something bad happens and you want to avoid it, you can spend Luck. The Keeper determines how many boxes you need to spend.
- **Harm track**: Shows how hurt you are. When a monster damages you, the Keeper marks Harm. If your Harm reaches "unstable" (marked by your playbook), you enter a weakened state and must choose an improvement to recover.
- **Experience track**: You gain Experience when you roll a miss (6 or lower) on any 2d6 roll. At 5 Experience, you level up and choose an improvement.
- **Moves**: Your special abilities and the situations that trigger them. Tap a move to see its full text.
- **Gear**: What you're carrying. The Keeper may ask you to justify having something; if you do, mark it down.
- **Notes**: A space for character details, backstory, or notes about ongoing plot threads.

### Editing Your Character

Tap the Luck, Harm, or Experience track to mark boxes forward (tap the last box to undo). Tap the Notes field to add or update character notes. All edits save automatically to your device and sync to the server when you're online.

### Leveling Up

When you reach 5 Experience, a "Level Up" prompt appears. Choose one improvement from the list:

- **Add a rating**: Bump one rating up by 1 (capped at +3).
- **Add a move**: Learn a new move from your playbook or another playbook (chosen at level up time).
- **Retire this hunter or mark experience**: End this character's story, or mark down your experience on your character sheet as lore.

Each improvement can only be taken once, but you can take them in any order. Basic improvements must be exhausted before you can take advanced ones.

## Rolling the Dice

When your hunter takes action in a tense situation, you roll 2d6 and add the relevant rating:

- **10+**: A full success. The Keeper tells you what happens, and you get what you wanted.
- **7-9**: A mixed success. You succeed, but there's a complication or cost.
- **6-**: A miss. Something bad happens, and you gain 1 Experience.

The move's text describes the specific outcome for each band. Tap the "Roll" button on any move to roll for it; the result banner shows your total, the outcome band, and the move's text for that outcome.

Your rolls are kept in a local history on your character sheet; this is for your reference only and isn't shared with the table.

## Playing Offline

MOWC works offline. When you're at the table without internet, your app continues to work:

- Roll dice, edit your character, mark Harm and Luck.
- Changes save to your phone or device immediately.
- When you go back online, changes automatically sync to the server and other players see them.

If you lose internet mid-session, just keep playing. Your Keeper will also be playing offline, and everything will catch up when the connection returns.

## Syncing and Online Play

When you're online, changes made by other players (like Keeper reveals or another hunter's edits) appear in real time. You'll see a sync status badge that shows online/offline and any pending changes.

If you see a notification that a change couldn't sync, tap the manual sync button. Most of the time, the app handles this automatically.

## Tips

- **Read your moves carefully.** The trigger and outcome are everything; don't second-guess when they apply.
- **Communicate with your Keeper.** If you're not sure whether something requires a roll, ask.
- **Keep notes on clues.** Use the Notes field to track anything your Keeper reveals that might help solve the mystery.
- **Respect the fiction.** MOTW is about telling a story together. Your character should act within the world, not like they have access to information they don't.

## Troubleshooting

**I can't find my campaign.** Check that you're logged in as the correct user. Campaigns appear under "My Campaigns" if you have a seat in them (i.e., you've been invited or created them).

**My character isn't showing up.** Make sure you created it in the correct campaign. You must create a new character for each campaign.

**My edits aren't syncing.** Check the sync status badge. If it shows "offline," the app will sync as soon as you reconnect. If you see a sync error, tap the manual sync button.

**I accidentally marked Harm/Luck.** Tap the last marked box to undo it.

**I filled the Harm track and it says "unstable."** You've crossed the unstable threshold. At the next opportunity, you must take an improvement to recover (this clears your Harm). Your Keeper will guide you through this.
